Executive Summary

Rate-led revenue outperformance absorbed by PIP-related expense pressure; March EBITDA still beat prior year.

March delivered $150K of Total Revenue, 6.7% ahead of budget and 10.9% above PY, at 80.2% occupancy and an ADR of $214 (+12.8% vs budget). Quarter-to-date revenue of $374K beat plan by 7.8% and PY by 10.5%. The EBITDA line tells a different story — March $26K landed $14K below budget but $4K above PY; Q1 $38K landed $17K below budget and $2K below PY. The driver is expense pressure tied to the active Property Improvement Plan, with accounting currently reviewing whether portions of Professional Fees and Property Operations & Maintenance should reclassify from operating to capital. On an operating basis, the commercial engine is working as designed — rate is moving, STR share is at an all-time high (March RGI 134.8), and mid-stay demand is expanding.

PIP Context · Important framing

ROOST Midtown is mid-way through an active Property Improvement Plan. Accounting is actively reviewing whether certain March and Q1 expense items — notably Professional Fees inside A&G and project-related spend inside Property Operations & Maintenance — should be reclassified from operating expense to capital. The P&L variances shown in this deck reflect current GAAP treatment and have not yet been reclassified. Two line items carry most of the operating EBITDA gap this quarter: A&G Professional Fees ran $13K over budget YTD and POM ran $11K over (March alone carried $8K of the POM variance). If reclassification occurs, the Q1 EBITDA variance to budget narrows materially. The headline framing here is operational: revenue is outperforming on a rate-led basis; the expense overrun is concentrated in PIP-adjacent categories that are explicitly under review.

The one-line read

Commercial performance is decisively ahead of plan — rate up 12.8% vs budget, STR share at peak, mid-stay demand accelerating — and operating EBITDA pressure is concentrated in Professional Fees and POM line items that are part of the ongoing PIP. March still beat PY on EBITDA (+18%) despite absorbing that pressure, and April MTD is pacing even stronger (RGI 136.7). The watch items into Q2 are (1) resolving the PIP capitalization review so the P&L cleanly reflects operating performance, (2) holding rate discipline as occupancy compresses, and (3) converting the mid-stay pipeline into bookings that backfill the 90+ night tail that rolled off YoY.

What drove the quarter

Performance highlights

  • Rate-led revenue beat. March ADR $214 ran 12.8% ahead of budget; Q1 ADR $200 ran ~5% ahead of plan on a smaller total base. Revenue beats are entirely rate-driven — occupancy trailed plan by 5.3 pts in March.
  • STR share at all-time high. March MPI 124.4, ARI 108.4, RGI 134.8 — the asset is the demand-and-rate leader in the Philadelphia CBD UUP comp set. Q1 RGI 135.6; running-12 RGI 121.0.
  • Mid-stay expansion. 7–29 night revenue +47% YoY; 30–89 night revenue +183% YoY. Curtis Institute, Philadelphia Orchestra, UPENN, and three new relocation-housing accounts are driving the shift.
  • Rooms labor discipline. March rooms labor 10.6% of Rooms Revenue, Q1 14.1% — both well inside industry norm of 18–25%. Flex-labor model absorbed the volume mix efficiently.

Three items to manage into Q2

Where the deck's focus lands

PIP expense review. A&G Professional Fees Q1 $15K vs $2K budget (+$13K) and POM $25K vs $14K (+$11K) are the two line items absorbing ~$24K of budget EBITDA. Finalizing the capitalization review in Q2 is the single largest reported-EBITDA lever.

90+ night tail roll-off. Extended-stay (90+ days) revenue fell -75% YoY as a 2025 long-stay block aged out. The mid-stay expansion has offset most of it; rebuilding one or two 90+ day anchors is the Q2 commercial target.

Hold rate discipline. March ADR $214 vs comp $197 (ARI 108.4) — the asset is pricing above its set. Sustaining that premium while lifting occupancy toward budget is how the EBITDA line reconnects with plan.

Revenue & Expense Drivers

What actually moved March & Q1
Rate · Mix · PIP cost overlay
Drivers · March & Q1 YTD

Rate drove revenue; PIP-adjacent categories drove the expense overrun.

The Q1 revenue beat is entirely rate-driven — ADR +$10 vs plan and +8.7% vs PY, absorbing occupancy that was flat to budget. Expenses above GOP are compressed by two line items (Professional Fees, POM) that are PIP-adjacent; every other Undistributed category landed at or below plan. The commercial engine is functioning; the reported EBITDA gap is a capitalization-review question, not an operating deterioration.

Revenue drivers

Where the top line beat originated

  • ADR premium. March ADR $214 (+12.8% vs budget, +13.8% vs PY); Q1 ADR $200 (+5.0% vs budget, +8.7% vs PY). Rate discipline is holding through the PIP period.
  • STR leadership. ARI 108.4 in March means the asset is pricing above the comp set — a structural position, not a discount play.
  • Mid-stay revenue growth. 7–29 night +47% YoY; 30–89 night +183% YoY. Middle-of-curve revenue is absorbing most of the 90+ day roll-off.
  • Net-new account pipeline. Six net-new Top-15 accounts contributed $49K in Q1 (ALE, SilverDoor, Jefferson, Guthrie, Aires, Packard Grande).
  • Other Revenue uplift. $8.6K YTD (+18.6% vs plan) on ancillary fees and parking / pet attachment.
  • April pacing. RGI 136.7 MTD (through April 20) — the trajectory is accelerating, not reverting.

Expense drivers

What produced the Q1 expense overrun

  • A&G Professional Fees. Q1 $15K vs $2K budget (+$13K). Largest single line-item variance — under PIP capitalization review.
  • Property Operations & Maintenance. Q1 $25K vs $14K (+$11K). March alone carried $8K of that — PIP-adjacent project work under review.
  • Property Taxes. Q1 $111K vs $99K (+$12K). Assessment-related variance; not PIP-driven but part of the Q1 fixed-cost pressure.
  • Sales & Marketing. Q1 $31K vs $34K plan (-$2K). Discipline through the PIP period.
  • Info & Telecom. Q1 $9K vs $9K (-$0.7K). At plan.
  • Utilities. Q1 $12K vs $12K. Effectively flat to plan.
  • Rooms labor. Q1 $42K vs $41K (+$1K). Department-level payroll control held against the commercial volume.
PIP capitalization bridge

If the Q1 variances in Professional Fees ($13K) and POM ($11K) reclassify in whole to capital, Q1 operating EBITDA moves from $38K reported to approximately $62K — about $7K above the $55K plan. If only half reclassifies, Q1 operating EBITDA is approximately $50K — essentially at plan. The capitalization review is the single most material presentation-layer item for the Q1 earnings story; the underlying operating performance (revenue +7.8% vs budget, controllable opex at or below plan, Rooms margin 86%) is favorable.

Bottom line

The commercial engine is working; the expense story is bifurcated. Rate outperformance, STR leadership, mid-stay expansion, and net-new account pipeline all read positive. Controllable operating expenses (S&M, IT, Utilities, Rooms Labor) are at or below plan. The reported EBITDA miss is concentrated in two PIP-adjacent line items that are explicitly under capitalization review. Resolution of that review is the single largest lever to reported Q2 EBITDA.
